Manchester United star gives seal of approval as Bryan Mbeumo transfer agreed

Manchester United goalkeeper Andre Onana appears to have given his seal of approval to the impending arrival of Brentford winger Bryan Mbeumo this summer.

On Thursday, the Manchester Evening News reported that United had submitted a third offer for Mbeumo. The price was believed to be £65m plus an additional £5m in add-ons.

The Manchester Evening News understood that there was confidence in the United camp that this bid would be accepted. Less than 24 hours later, that is the case.

Varying reports have suggested that the Bees have agreed to allow Mbeumo to leave for a fee that could reach £70m. Among those to report this news was journalist Fabrizio Romano.

He posted on Instagram that the 'saga' was now over and that Brentford had given United the green light to finalise their approach for Mbeumo. It is claimed that the contract with Mbeumo was agreed 'weeks ago' and, as the Manchester Evening News has reported previously, the Cameroonian only wanted United.

Among those to like the post was Onana, who is understood to have played a role in the capture of Mbeumo. In June, we reported that the goalkeeper had been advising his international teammate to move to Old Trafford this summer.

This is despite reports linking him with an exit. The Manchester Evening News believes that Onana is focused and committed to staying at United even though AS Monaco have been linked with his signature.

With Brentford having accepted the Mbeumo bid - as reported by the Manchester Evening News - the transfer could be wrapped up sooner rather than later. On Tuesday, the Reds will fly out to America for their pre-season tour but not before they face Leeds in a friendly on Saturday afternoon.
