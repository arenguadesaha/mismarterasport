Manchester United face immediate Bryan Mbeumo dilemma as £70m transfer agreed

Isaac is a senior NCTJ-trained journalist with an MA in News Journalism. He has experience of working for national and regional news organisations including the Express, the Manchester Evening News and the Liverpool Echo. He currently covers sport across all regional sites at Reach PLC.

Manchester United are closing in on the signing of Bryan Mbeumo after reaching an agreement with Brentford.

The Manchester Evening News understands that the Reds have had a bid accepted, bringing an end to one of the summer's longest-running transfer sagas. United will pay the Bees £65million plus £5m in add-ons for their 25-year-old forward, who could now join before the club's pre-season tour of the United States next week.

Mbeumo's talents are obvious with a record of 20 goals in the top-flight last season. The Cameroonian will provide United with the Premier League experience they have been looking to attract this summer after acquiring Matheus Cunha from Wolverhampton Wanderers.

But it's his record from the spot which should draw the most attention. Mbeumo has taken 12 penalties in the Premier League since joining Brentford in 2019 - scoring 11 and missing just one.

That sole miss came in what is set to be his final home game for the Bees - a 3-2 defeat to Fulham on May 18. It's to his credit though that he still managed to score that day and in his final game for the club - a 1-1 draw at Wolves.

And his almost spotless record from the spot will now present United with a welcome but immediate dilemma heading into the new campaign. Reds captain Bruno Fernandes has been United's regular penalty taker since joining from Sporting in January 2020.

Fernandes has scored 22 Premier League penalties for the Reds - missing just four. It seems unlikely that United boss Ruben Amorim will change his regular penalty taker but it certainly gives him some room for thought.

It's a good problem to have for Amorim and he will be pleased to have at least one thing going for him heading into a vital campaign for him and the Reds.

Mbeumo's arrival won't fix much of United's problems but his signing alongside Cunha's addition last month will at least add some much-needed firepower to a team which badly needs it.
