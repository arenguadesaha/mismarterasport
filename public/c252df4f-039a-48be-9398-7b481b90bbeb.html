Marcus Rashford supported by Man United teammates as he sends message ahead of Leeds

Marcus Rashford has posted on social media hours before Manchester United play Leeds United in their first pre-season friendly of the summer.

United did not include Rashford in the 29-player travelling squad for the game in Stockholm, Sweden. He is one of five senior players - Alejandro Garnacho, Antony, Jadon Sancho and Tyrell Malacia completing the quintet - currently not training with the first team.

All five have informed the club they wish to explore opportunities elsewhere. Rashford would favour a move overseas after spending the second half of last season on loan in the Premier League at Aston Villa.

The player has now shared more photos on Instagram showing him working at a gym. "The lab," the post's caption reads.

It is the third such one featuring footage of Rashford working out since the start of last week. Several different United players have acknowledged these posts.

Antony, Diogo Dalot, Ethan Wheatley, Godwill Kikonki, Joe Hugill and Joshua Zirkzee all liked the latest one.

The former, like Rashford, is not in the squad to face Leeds. Neither are Ethan Wheatley, Joe Hugill or Joshua Zirkzee.

Hugill is not after joining Barnet on a season-long loan that same day. United, meanwhile, say Zirkzee has a "small issue".

Several of the club's former players have also shown Rashford their support. They include Aaron Wan-Bissaka, Brandon Williams, Donny van de Beek, Facundo Pellistri and Jesse Lingard. Andre Onana, Bruno Fernandes, Jadon Sancho, Lisandro Martinez, Matheus Cunha, Matthijs de Ligt and Rasmus Hojlund have liked previous posts.

Rashford has previously posted footage of him working on running tracks and playing padel this summer. "The work don't stop! Let's go," he captioned a video of himself at the former. Then last month, an update read: "Putting in the work with family and friends before the serious stuff starts back up."
