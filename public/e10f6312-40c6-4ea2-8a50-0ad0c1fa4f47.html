Manchester United await Jadon Sancho update as '£26m transfer agreed' amid Juventus interest

Italian giants Juventus are reportedly set for a £26million cash injection amid ongoing reports linking them with a transfer swoop for Manchester United forward Jadon Sancho.

The 25-year-old winger, along with Marcus Rashford, Antony, Alejandro Garnacho, and Tyrell Malacia, is surplus to requirments at Old Trafford. Juventus are reportedly leading the race for his signature, which has also seen links to Napoli and Fenerbahce, and potential swap deals suggested.

Juventus have reportedly touted the likes of Tim Weah, Douglas Luiz, and Dusan Vlahovic as possible player exhanges for the United star, but there's not been a breakthrough in negotiations yet. However, the Old Lady may soon have plenty of funds to complete a cash deal instead.

Gazzetta dello Sport claim that the Turin club have agreed a deal to sell forward Nico Gonzalez to Al Ahli for £26m. The funds received could then be used to sign Sancho.

Napoli sporting director Giovanni Manna, meanwhile, has appeared to suggest a switch to his side will not happen unless he lowers his existing demands.

"Our offer is clear, we will not pay Sancho's requested salary," he said last month via journalist Yagiz Sabuncuoglu on X.

The Manchester Evening News understands that Sancho's representative, Emeka Obasi, has explored potential opportunities in Germany following his time at Dortmund - with his most recent spell coming during the second half of the 2023/2024 campaign. However, a move to the Bundesliga is no longer being considered.

United want to secure a similar fee to the one that was agreed with Chelsea, in the region of £25million, despite what was a largely disappointing spell in west London.

Sancho scored five goals and provided 10 assists in 41 matches for the Blues, who ended the season by winning the Club World Cup, UEFA Conference League and finishing in the Premier League top four.
