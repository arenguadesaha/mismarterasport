Man United next transfer after Bryan Mbeumo to complete £200m outlay is clear

Isaac is a senior NCTJ-trained journalist with an MA in News Journalism. He has experience of working for national and regional news organisations including the Express, the Manchester Evening News and the Liverpool Echo. He currently covers sport across all regional sites at Reach PLC.

Manchester United have submitted an improved offer to sign Brentford forward Bryan Mbeumo over a month on from their original approach. United have signed just two players this summer and know they'll need to strengthen their squad further if they want to avoid a repeat of last season.

The Reds activated Matheus Cunha's £62.5million release clause early last month, bringing the Wolves forward to Old Trafford on a five-year deal with an option for an extra year. Paraguayan left-back Diego Leon has also joined Ruben Amorim's side for a fee that could rise to £7m.

And the Reds are now stepping up their efforts to complete their third summer signing ahead of the club's pre-season tour of the United States. The Manchester Evening News understands that United's third bid for the Cameroonian is £65m plus £5m in add-ons.

It is understood that there is confidence in the bid being accepted by Brentford. Attention will then turn to who is next as Amorim seeks reinforcements for his underperforming squad.

Midfield will need strengthening due to Casemiro's age, Mason Mount's injury record and Christian Eriksen's departure. Crystal Palace's Adam Wharton is one of the brightest English midfielders around at the moment.

Wharton is a Premier League regular for the Eagles, who played a huge role in their shock FA Cup win. The 21-year-old is also an England international who has been to a major tournament with the Three Lions. Ineos appears to be targeting young stars at Old Trafford, and he fits the bill perfectly.

Palace are said to value their No. 20 at up to £60m, having signed him from Blackburn Rovers for £22m just over a year ago. The young star is reportedly attracting interest from Tottenham Hotspur so the Reds may need to act fast to secure his signature.

If United were to complete deals for Wharton and Mbeumo this summer, the total outlay on transfer fees could rise to around £200m.

Attention will also need to be paid to outgoings as the club looks to continue complying with the Premier League's Profit and Sustainability Rules (PSR).

The Reds are also yet to shift anyone from their squad, despite Marcus Rashford, Antony, Tyrell Malacia, Jadon Sancho, and Alejandro Garnacho being deemed surplus to requirements by Amorim.
