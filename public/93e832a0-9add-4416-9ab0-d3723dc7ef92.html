Marcus Rashford Man United transfer latest - Barcelona offer, pre-season decision, wages update

One name who has been talked about a lot over the summer transfer window so far is Manchester United academy graduate Marcus Rashford.

It seems an expected move away from Old Trafford may now be edging closer with the latest reports over his future. The forward last appeared for United in the Europa League fixture against Viktoria Plzen on December 12, and that now looks to be his final appearance for the club.

It is reported that Spanish giants Barcelona are working on a move for the 27-year-old with the player keen on a move to the Nou Camp. Barca are keen on signing a wide player this summer with a deal for Nico Williams falling through after he opted to stay with Athletic Club.

It means that Barcelona have now turned their attention back to Rashford, with progress seemingly being made on a deal. Here is the latest on the player’s future and a potential move to Spain.

In the latest big update, the Athletic report that Barcelona are now ‘advancing’ in a move to sign Rashford this summer, having made an offer to United. The offer will see the forward sign for the club on loan for the season with the agreement having an option to buy included.

The report says that United are open to that idea, although it is not yet a done deal, talks are said to be at the final stages.

Barcelona are a club who have been liked with an interest for some time. The loan deal will see Barcelona negotiate their financial situation.

Rashford has sounded out Spanish intermediaries to try and engineer a move to the Catalan side

The update comes after Rashford has not been involved in training with the squad for pre-season. He instead is on an individual training programme.

He is using the facilities at United’s Carrington training ground but arrives on site after the first team has left following their session. He is not part of the travelling squad for the pre-season friendly against Leeds United this afternoon.

Rashford spent the second half of last season on loan with Aston Villa, and the Athletic now give more information on the wage contribution involved in that deal. It’s reported that he is on more than £325,000 per-week, with Villa covering 75 percent as well as performance-related bonuses during the loan deal, with United covering the other 25 percent

Villa did have the option of making the move permanent for a £40 million fee this summer, but they decided not to take up that option.

Speaking about the possibility of signing a new wide man back in May, Flick said: This is what we have talked about with the club. In the last matches maybe we did not have a lot of options in that position… so we have to bring more options to the team and more quality. This is the job from Deco, is clear, and I will support him.”

When asked about Luis Diaz and Rashford specifically, he added: “I don’t want to speak about players which are not in my team, but of course Luis Diaz and Marcus Rashford are fantastic players. I like them and we’ll see what happens.”
