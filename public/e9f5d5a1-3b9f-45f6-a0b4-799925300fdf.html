Why Harry Amass is not in the Manchester United squad vs Leeds

Manchester United left back Harry Amass is set to go on loan for the forthcoming season.

Amass, 18, has been omitted from United's travelling squad for their friendly against Leeds United in Stockholm on Saturday and he will not be included in their pre-season tour squad that flies to Chicago next week.

The absence of the left back from United's plans for the forthcoming campaign will raise the possibility of United moving for a left-sided target.

United bought Patrick Dorgu from Lecce for £25million rising to £29m in January and the only specialist alternative is Luke Shaw, who has played 27 times in the past two seasons due to numerous injury issues.

Shaw turned 30 recently and is into his 12th year at United. He is under contract until 2027.

Paraguayan teenager Diego Leon recently completed his move to United but it is unclear how involved he will be in the senior squad this season.

Amass joined United from Watford's academy in 2023 and he excelled on the pre-season tour of the United States last year. However, he was surprisingly omitted from the squad for the Community Shield against Manchester City and United finished the game with Facundo Pellistri, a right winger, at left back.

Londoner Amass eventually made his debut against Leicester City in March.
