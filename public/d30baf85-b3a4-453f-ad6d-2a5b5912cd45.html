Manchester United finally reach £70m agreement to seal Bryan Mbeumo transfer

Manchester United have reached an agreement with Brentford to sign attacker Bryan Mbeumo in a deal that could be worth up to £70million.

It is understood United will pay £65m up front and up to a further £5m in add-ons. United's most recent offer for the Cameroonian was their third bid of the transfer window.

United, who entered talks with Brentford to sign Mbeumo in early-June, have always maintained a sense of confidence they would reach an agreement with the Londoners.

After signing Matheus Cunha to strengthen their attack last month, United immediately identified Mbeumo as their next top target. The club has prioritised attacking signings this summer after finishing each of the last two Premier League campaigns with a negative goal difference.

Mbeumo, who scored 20 Premier League goals last season, has been keen on a move to Old Trafford throughout the summer, meaning both he and United have been keen to strike an agreement.

United submitted an opening offer for the former Troyes man worth £55m, which was rejected, and their second proposal was worth in excess of £60m just over three weeks ago.

For a time, it looked as though United's second offer would be enough to extract Mbeumo from the Gtech Community Stadium.

The attacker, who was directly involved in 28 Premier League goals last season, will bring plenty of versatility to Old Trafford. As well as being an option at right-wing and for the right-sided No.10 role, he is also capable of playing through the centre of the attack.

As reported by the Manchester Evening News earlier this week, United have been trying to get a deal in place for Mbeumo to ensure he can feature on the upcoming pre-season tour of the United States.

United will fly to Chicago next Tuesday before facing West Ham United in their opening game of the Premier League Summer Series on July 27 at the MetLife Stadium in New Jersey.
