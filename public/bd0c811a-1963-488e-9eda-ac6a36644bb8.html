Manchester United make decision on Diego Leon future after summer transfer

Diego Leon is set to be given the chance to secure a place in the Manchester United first team squad for the forthcoming season.

Well-placed sources say coaching staff have been impressed by left back Leon in training and he could make his first appearance for United in their pre-season friendly against Leeds United on Saturday.

Leon, 18, has been present for the entirety of United's pre-season schedule so far and is set to be included in their pre-season tour squad that flies to Chicago on Tuesday.

Eighteen-year-old left back Harry Amass has been omitted from the travelling squad for the Leeds match and will not travel to the United States as he is set to head out on loan.

United signed left winger Patrick Dorgu from Lecce for £25million in January and there is no obvious cover for the Denmark international other than Leon.

Luke Shaw has played only 27 times in the past two seasons for United amid innumerable injury issues and head coach Ruben Amorim is expected to primarily use him at centre back.

United completed the signing of Paraguay Under 20 international Leon from Cerro Porteño earlier this month, having agreed a deal last season.
